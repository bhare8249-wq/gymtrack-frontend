01 🚀 Getting Started

Barbell Labs is a workout tracker built for everyone who trains — gym, home, garage, or anywhere you break a sweat. Getting started takes less than 60 seconds.

Creating Your Account

Open the app and tap "Create Account" Enter a username, your email address, and a password (min. 8 characters, 1 uppercase, 1 lowercase, 1 number).
Verify your email A verification link is sent to your inbox. Tap it to confirm your account. Check spam if you don't see it.
Sign in and set up your profile Go to the Profile tab → tap Edit to add your name, age, weight, height, city, and training goal.
Start logging Tap the Log tab, hit "Add Exercise", pick from the 224-exercise library, and record your first set.
💡 Tip You can also sign in with Google for one-tap account creation — no password required.

02 📋 Logging a Workout

The Log tab is where you record every set, rep, and weight. Everything you log today becomes the coaching and charts you see tomorrow.

Starting a Workout

Tap the Log tab Or press "Start Workout" on the Home tab to jump straight into logging.
Tap "Add Exercise" Opens the exercise picker. Search by name, or filter by muscle group and equipment type.
Log your sets Enter weight and reps for each set. Tap "+ Add Set" to add more. Tap the trash icon to remove a set.
Tap "Finish Workout" when done The app saves your session, calculates duration, updates your streak, and shows your post-workout summary.

Set Fields

FieldDescription
WeightThe load used. Enter 0 or leave blank for bodyweight exercises.
RepsThe number of reps completed in that set.
RPERate of Perceived Exertion (1–10). How hard the set felt. Tap to toggle.
RIRReps in Reserve. How many more reps you had left. Tap to toggle.
NotesFreeform text for any set — form cues, how you felt, variations used.

Workout Labels

Tag each workout with up to 3 labels (Legs, Push, Pull, Upper Body, Lower Body, Shoulders, Arms, Abs, Glutes). Labels appear in History to help you spot patterns.

⚡ Quick Log On the Home tab, tap "Quick Log Last Session" to instantly reload your most recent workout as a starting point. Edit any sets you want to change, then finish.

04 🤖 AI Coach

After every set, Barbell Labs analyses your entire history for that exercise and shows you a personalised recommendation — exactly what weight and reps to aim for next.

How It Works

📈 Progressive Overload

If you've been consistent and RPE was moderate, the coach nudges up weight or reps to drive adaptation.

🔄 Plateau Detection

Stalled at the same weight for 3+ sessions? The coach tells you it's time to break through — or suggests a deload.

🩹 Recovery Mode

Coming back after time off? The coach eases you back in with a reduced target to protect against injury.

💪 RIR & RPE Aware

Logged high RIR (lots in the tank)? The coach pushes you harder. Logged RPE 9–10? It may suggest backing off slightly.

💡 Tip The more sessions you log, the smarter the coach becomes. After 3–5 sessions on an exercise you'll start seeing highly accurate recommendations.

Using Suggestions

The AI recommendation appears as a banner above the set rows. Tap Apply to pre-fill the weight and reps into your next set, or Dismiss to ignore it for this session.

05 ⏱ Rest Timer

The built-in rest timer sits inside every exercise block. It keeps your rest periods consistent and signals you when it's time to go again.

Using the Timer

Choose a preset Tap 30s, 1m, 2m, 3m, 5m — or tap Custom to enter any minutes:seconds combination.
Tap Start The timer counts down. You'll feel a haptic vibration when it hits zero.
Resume or Reset Tap Pause to stop mid-rest. Tap Reset to cancel and start fresh.
🔔 Haptic Feedback When the timer reaches zero, your phone vibrates to alert you even if your screen is off or you're mid-conversation.
06 🎯 RPE & RIR Tracking

Track effort, not just weight. RPE and RIR give you and the AI Coach the full picture of how hard each set really was.

MetricScaleWhat it means
RPE
Rate of Perceived Exertion		1–10How hard the set felt. 10 = absolute max effort, no reps left. 7 = moderately hard, a few left.
RIR
Reps in Reserve		0–5+How many more reps you could have done. 0 = failure. 3 = three reps left in the tank.

RPE Quick Reference

RPEFeelRIR Equiv.
10Max effort — couldn't do another rep0
9Could squeeze out 1 more rep1
8Hard — 2 reps left2
7Moderate — 3 reps left3
6Comfortable — could go much harder4+
💡 Tip Log RPE and RIR consistently and the AI Coach will use that data to make progressively smarter recommendations.
07 📈 Progress & Charts

The Progress tab shows dual-line charts for every exercise you've logged — weight and reps plotted together over your entire history.

Reading Your Charts

📊 Dual-Line Chart

Each exercise graph shows two lines: weight (solid, steel blue) and reps (dashed, lighter). Both rising together = ideal progression.

🔍 Scrubbing

Drag your finger along the chart to scrub through history session by session. The tooltip shows exact weight, reps, and date for each point.

👑 1RM Estimate Badge

Each exercise card shows your estimated one-rep max (Epley + Brzycki average). Tap the "1RM" button in Log to get a full percentage table.

💡 The longer you log, the more powerful it gets. After 10+ sessions per exercise, trends and plateaus become clearly visible.
08 🏆 My Top Lifts

The top of the Progress tab shows your personal records for your three most important exercises. By default these are Barbell Bench Press, Barbell Back Squat, and Conventional Deadlift — but you can change them to any exercise in the library.

Customising Your Top Lifts

Tap "Edit" next to My Top Lifts The three lift slots expand into individual pickers.
Tap any slot to change it A search field appears. Type to filter all 224 exercises. Tap one to select it.
Tap "Save" Your choices are saved to your profile and synced across devices.
👑 Crown Badge The lift with your highest PR across all three receives a "TOP PR" crown badge. Chase it every session.
09 💯 1RM Estimator

Your estimated one-rep max tells you your theoretical maximum for a lift based on what you actually lifted, without ever having to max out.

Accessing the 1RM Calculator

In the Log tab, tap the 1RM pill button in the top-right corner. Enter any weight and rep count to instantly see your estimated 1RM.

Percentage Table

Below your 1RM estimate, a full percentage table shows training loads from 50% to 100% — helping you program exactly the right weight for any rep range.

% of 1RMRep RangeTraining Zone
90–100%1–3 repsMax strength / peaking
75–89%4–8 repsStrength + hypertrophy
60–74%8–15 repsHypertrophy
50–59%15+ repsEndurance / warmup
🔢 Formula Uses the average of the Epley and Brzycki formulas for maximum accuracy across all rep ranges.
10 🏋️ Plate Calculator

Know exactly which plates to load before you even walk up to the bar. Tap Plates in the Log tab header.

How to Use

Select your unit (lbs or kg) Toggle between imperial and metric with the unit switch.
Choose your bar weight Standard 45lb / 20kg, Women's 35lb / 15kg, or EZ-Bar 25lb / 11kg.
Enter your target weight The calculator instantly shows which plates go on each side, with a colour-coded visual bar diagram.
🎨 Colour Coding Each plate size has its own colour matching standard gym plate colours — red 25s, blue 20s, yellow 15s, green 10s, white 5s, and so on.
11 📄 Workout Templates

Save any completed workout as a template and reload it in one tap on any future session.

Saving a Template

Complete a workout On the post-workout summary screen, tap "Save as Template".
Name your template Give it a memorable name — "Push Day A", "Leg Day", "Upper Body Volume", etc.
Tap Save The template is stored in your account and synced to all devices.

Loading a Template

In the Log tab, tap Templates to open your saved list. Tap any template to load it as your current workout — all exercises and set structure pre-filled. You just enter the weights and reps.

Managing Templates

Open the Templates sheet to rename or delete any template. Changes sync immediately.

12 💪 Muscle Group Breakdown

See exactly which muscle groups you've been training — and which ones you might be neglecting — over the last 7 days, 30 days, or full year.

Reading the Breakdown

📊 Proportional Bar Chart

Each muscle group shows a bar sized proportionally to the number of sets you logged. Bigger bar = more volume that muscle received.

🗓 Time Ranges

Toggle between 7D (last 7 days), 30D (last 30 days), and 1Y (last year) to spot weekly imbalances vs. long-term patterns.

💡 Use this to catch imbalances early. If your push muscles (chest, shoulders, triceps) are dominating over pull muscles (back, biceps), adjust your programming before it causes issues.
13 📖 Workout History

Every workout you've ever completed lives in the History tab — fully searchable, expandable, and exportable.

Browsing History

Sessions are listed newest first. Tap any session card to expand the full exercise list with every set, weight, and rep. Workout labels appear as coloured badges on each card.

Exporting to CSV

Tap the Export CSV button in the History tab to download all your workout data as a CSV file. Open it in Excel, Google Sheets, or any spreadsheet app for custom analysis. Your data is always yours.

📊 What's included in the export Date, workout duration, exercise name, set number, weight, reps, RPE, RIR, and any notes you logged.
14 ⚖️ Body Weight Tracking

Log your body weight over time to track long-term trends alongside your training data.

Logging Body Weight

Go to the Progress tab and scroll to the Body Weight section. Enter today's weight and tap Save. A running chart shows your trend over time.

💡 Best practice Weigh yourself first thing in the morning, after using the bathroom, and before eating or drinking. Consistency in conditions makes the trend line meaningful.
15 🔥 Streak Tracking

Your streak counts consecutive days with at least one completed workout. It's displayed prominently on the Home tab to keep the habit alive.

How Streaks Work

📅 Daily counting

Log at least one workout per day to extend your streak. Missing a day resets it to zero.

🔥 Streak display

Your current streak is shown as an orange badge on the Home tab. The number updates immediately after finishing a workout.

💡 Rest days are part of the plan. A streak tracks training frequency — even a short 15-minute session counts. Active recovery work counts too.

17 ☁️ Cloud Sync & Export

Your data is stored in Firebase Firestore and synced in real time across all your devices — no manual backup required.

How Sync Works

⚡ Real-time sync

Every workout, profile change, and template is saved instantly to the cloud. Sign in on any device and your full history is there immediately.

📱 Multiple devices

Use Barbell Labs on your phone, tablet, and desktop simultaneously. All devices stay in sync automatically.

CSV Export

To export your data, go to the History tab → Export CSV. The file includes every set from every workout ever logged. No lock-in — your data is always portable.

💡 Data ownership Barbell Labs never sells or shares your data. You own it completely. Export anytime and take it anywhere.
18 🔒 Account & Security

Manage your account credentials from the Profile tab → Settings → Security.

Changing Your Email

Go to Profile → Settings → Security → Change Email Enter your current password to verify your identity.
Enter your new email address A verification link is sent to the new address.
Verify the new address Click the link in the email. Your account email updates immediately.

Changing Your Password

Profile → Settings → Security → Change Password. Enter your current password, then your new password (same requirements as signup: min. 8 chars, 1 uppercase, 1 lowercase, 1 digit).

Signing Out

A Sign Out button is always visible at the bottom of the Profile tab. Tapping it signs you out and returns you to the login screen. Your data remains safely stored in the cloud.

Sign In with Google

If you created your account with Google, your password is managed through your Google account. Email/password changes are not available for Google accounts.

🔒 Security All data is encrypted in transit and at rest via Firebase. Barbell Labs never stores your password — it's handled entirely by Firebase Authentication.
